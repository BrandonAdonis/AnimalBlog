Brown Hare


The brownhare is the 10th fastest animal in the world

  • The Brownhare can run up to 77km/h using their powerful hind legs!
  • They do not use burrows like their close relatives the rabbit.
  • They live above ground and will make small depressions in the ground which they use for cover.
  • They rely on their incredible speed to keep out of grips of would-be predators such as foxes.

Blue Wildebeest


The Blue Wildebeest is the 9th fastest animal in the world

  • The Blue Wildebeest can run up to 80kph, although, unfortunately for them, they cannot run as fast as lions or cheetahs!
  • Their main defence from predators is sticking together in a herd. Wildebeest stick together and move in herds to be more safe from other predators.
  • There are around 1.5 million living in the Serengeti, They are widespread in many areas of Africa.
  • The blue wildebeest is a herbivore, feeding primarily on the short grasses which commonly grow on light, and alkaline soils that are found in savanna grasslands and on plains.

Marlin


The Marlin is the 8th fastest animal in the world

  • Marlins can swim and reach up to 80km/h!
  • There are around 10 species of marlin. They are also closely related to sword fish.
  • The Blue Marlin is one of the largest species of fish in the world!
  • Marlin have a long, sharp upper jaw which they use to slash and stun fish, making it alot easier for the marlin to catch

Pronghron Antilope


The Pronghron Antilope is the 7th fastest animal in the world

  • The Pronghron Antilope can run up to 98km/h using their powerful hind legs!
  • The Pronghron Antilope lives in grasslands, deserts, river basins, and just about any wide open space.
  • They live above ground and will make small depressions in the ground which they use for cover.
  • The Antilope needs its speed to out run the perdators since it lives in the grassland which is home to cheetahs, gazelles, lions, and tigers

Sail Fish


The Sail Fish is the 6th fastest animal in the world

  • The Sail Fish can swim up to 110km/h!
  • The Sail Fish lives in the Atlantic at depths of 200 m
  • They travel in groups, and they use their numbers to drive a school upwards, where the surface acts as a barrier.
  • The Sail fish requires the speed it has so it can outswim the larger predatory fish like open ocean shark species, orcas and dolphinfish

Cheetah


The Cheetah is the 5th fastest animal in the world

  • The Cheetah can run up to 113km/h which is 3x the speed of the fastest human top speed which was 36km/h!
  • Cheetahs live in the grasslands
  • The Cheetah is a carnivorous animal, they will only hunt in groups so that they can take down larger prey.
  • Lions, leopards, and hyenas will try to prey upon cheetahs, particularly cheetah cubs, because adult cheetahs are difficult to catch.

The Spur-Winged Goose


The The Spur-Winged Goose is the 4th fastest animal in the world

  • The Spur-Winged Goose can fly up to 142km/h using their powerful hind legs!
  • This animal is oftenly found in open grasslands with lakes, seasonal pools, rivers, swamps and river deltas.
  • They are a social bird and can be found gathering in groups consisting of as many as 50 individuals.
  • The Spur-Winged Geese are threatened by uncontrolled hunting, as in traditional Nigerian medicine, they are highly valued.

The Frigate Bird


The Frigate Bird is the 3rd fastest animal in the world

  • The Frigate Bird can reach up to to 153km/h!
  • The Frigate Bird is usually airbrone and can stay airbrone for more than a week since it has the largest wingspan to body weight ratio of any bird.
  • The Frigate birds live along coasts and islands in tropical and subtropical waters. They nest and roost in low trees and shrubs.
  • The bird does not have too many natural predators since they are a larger bird and spend most of their time airborne. However, there are a few predators that threaten them when they are on land, like Stoats, domestic cats, and rats may hunt for these birds or eat their eggs.

The White Throated Needletail


The White Throated Needletail is the 2nd fastest animal in the world

  • The White Throated Needletail is the fastest bird in powered flight, with a top recorded speeds of 171km/h!
  • This bird is a migratory bird, breeding in Central Asia and southern Siberia, and wintering south in the Indian Subcontinent, Southeast Asia and Australia.
  • The White-throated Needletail feeds on flying insects, such as termites, ants, beetles and flies.
  • The White Throated Needletail has no known predators or threats, however it is likely that populations of prey species have been and are being affected by pesticide use and air pollution.

The Peregrine Falcon


The Peregrine Falcon is the fastest animal in the world!

  • The Peregrine Falcon can reach up tp 322km/h when its hunting by flying to great heights, then dives steeply at speeds of over 322km/h. It also reaches up to 90km/h when normally flying!
  • The Peregrine falcon is among the world's most common birds of prey and live on all continents except Antarctica. They prefer wide-open spaces, and thrive near coasts where shorebirds are common, but they can be found everywhere from tundra to deserts.
  • Their typical prey items include shorebirds, ducks, gulls, pigeons, and songbirds. The Peregrine falcons also eat bats, and they occasionally steal prey.
  • Although the Peregrine Falcon is an elite predator, it does have its own predators, including Gyrfalcons, eagles, Great Horned owls, and other Peregrines.
